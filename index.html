Welcome to the course website for IoT Fundamentals. Explore the syllabus, weekly plans, resources, and more!

Course Overview

This course introduces students to the fundamentals of the Internet of Things (IoT), including its architecture, protocols, hardware platforms, and real-world applications. Through a blend of theoretical lectures (3 credits, 45 hours) and hands-on practical sessions (1 credit, 30 hours), students will explore IoT concepts such as sensing, actuators, communication models, and security, while gaining practical skills with platforms like Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and ESP8266. Duration: 15 weeks (Feb 26, 2025 - Jun 15, 2025). Prerequisites: Basic understanding of programming, computer networks, and hardware fundamentals.

Lesson Plan

Theory (3 hrs): Definition & Characteristics of IoT, Physical Design, Basic Components (Things, Sensing, Actuators). Activities: Lecture on IoT evolution, discussion on examples. Assessment: 5 MCQ quiz.
Practical (2 hrs): Study IoT software & components, install Arduino IDE. Activities: Install and explore IDE. Deliverable: Verified installation.

Theory (3 hrs): Physical Design of IoT, IoT Protocols, Vision, Physical Parameters. Activities: Lecture on device structure, case study on HTTP/CoAP/MQTT. Assessment: Group discussion.
Practical (2 hrs): Microcontroller setup on breadboard. Activities: Connect Arduino Uno with LED. Deliverable: Working circuit.

Theory (3 hrs): Application Areas (Home Automation, Smart Cities, Medical, etc.). Activities: Lecture with examples, video on smart cities. Assessment: 200-word summary assignment.
Practical (2 hrs): Analog Input/Output. Activities: Connect potentiometer and LED. Deliverable: Functional analog circuit.

Theory (3 hrs): Communication Models, APIs, 3-layer & 5-layer Architecture. Activities: Lecture on models, diagram exercise. Assessment: Sketch IoT layers.
Practical (2 hrs): LED Blinking with Arduino. Activities: Write code to blink LED. Deliverable: Blinking LED demo.

Theory (3 hrs): ITU IoT Model, M2M Communication, Gateways. Activities: Lecture on ITU model, discussion on gateways. Assessment: 10-question quiz.
Practical (2 hrs): Push Button/Digital Sensor Interfacing. Activities: Connect button to turn on LED. Deliverable: Working system.

Theory (3 hrs): BLE, ZigBee, LoRaWAN. Activities: Lecture on comparisons, debate on smart home protocols. Assessment: Short essay (due next week).
Practical (2 hrs): DHT11 Sensor Interfacing. Activities: Display temp/humidity on Serial Monitor. Deliverable: Sensor readings.

Theory (3 hrs): Review Units 1 & 2. Activities: Recap lecture, Q&A. Assessment: Mid-term exam (50 marks).
Practical (2 hrs): Open Lab - Troubleshooting. Activities: Debug previous experiments. Deliverable: Improved circuits.

Theory (3 hrs): EPC, NFC, 6LoWPAN. Activities: Lecture on applications, case study on NFC. Assessment: Discussion on NFC vs. BLE.
Practical (2 hrs): ThingSpeak Integration. Activities: Send dummy data to cloud. Deliverable: Visualized data.

Theory (3 hrs): MQTT, SCADA, Hardware Platforms. Activities: Lecture on MQTT, demo of Raspberry Pi vs. Arduino. Assessment: 300-word MQTT assignment.
Practical (2 hrs): Send Sensor Data to Cloud. Activities: Use DHT11 with ESP8266. Deliverable: Real-time dashboard.

Theory (3 hrs): Privacy & Security Issues, Secure IoT Steps. Activities: Lecture on vulnerabilities, group activity on security measures. Assessment: 5-min group presentation.
Practical (2 hrs): Motor Control with Relay. Activities: Turn motor ON with button. Deliverable: Working system.

Theory (3 hrs): Review Hardware Platforms. Activities: Lecture on platform strengths, discussion on project choices. Assessment: 10-question quiz.
Practical (2 hrs): Web Server with ESP8266. Activities: Toggle LED via webpage. Deliverable: Remote control demo.

Theory (3 hrs): Recap Protocols & Security. Activities: Recap MQTT/NFC, analyze breach scenario. Assessment: Suggest fixes exercise.
Practical (2 hrs): Data Logging to Cloud/SD. Activities: Log temp data, create graph. Deliverable: Visualized trends.

Theory (3 hrs): Review Unit 3. Activities: Recap MQTT/hardware/security, Q&A. Assessment: 20-mark practice test.
Practical (2 hrs): MQTT Setup. Activities: Use Mosquitto to publish/subscribe data. Deliverable: MQTT demo.

Theory (3 hrs): IoT Project Discussion. Activities: Brainstorm project ideas, assign groups. Assessment: 1-page proposal.
Practical (2 hrs): Soil Moisture System. Activities: Connect sensor, send data to cloud. Deliverable: Working system.

Theory (3 hrs): Course Review & Exam Prep. Activities: Recap Units 1-3, mock exam. Assessment: Q&A, feedback.
Practical (2 hrs): Home Automation System. Activities: Build relay-based system, present. Deliverable: Working demo.

Assessment Breakdown

  • Theory (3 Credits): Mid-Term Exam (20%), Assignments/Quizzes (20%), Final Exam (60%)
  • Practical (1 Credit): Lab Performance (50%), Final Project (50%) - Soil Moisture or Home Automation
